entered into a definitive merger agreement with Hess Corporation (&#8220;<span style="text-decoration: underline">Hess</span>&#8221;), pursuant to which, <span style="background-color: white">subject
to the terms and conditions of the merger agreement, a subsidiary of Chevron will be merged with and into Hess, with Hess continuing as
the surviving corporation of such merger and as a direct, wholly-owned subsidiary of Chevron (such transaction, the &#8220;</span><span style="text-decoration: underline">Merger</span><span style="background-color: white">&#8221;)</span>.</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0 0 12pt">Hess Guyana Exploration Limited (&#8220;<span style="text-decoration: underline">HGEL</span>&#8221;), a
wholly owned subsidiary of Hess, is currently in arbitration with respect to the right of first refusal (the &#8220;<span style="text-decoration: underline">Stabroek ROFR</span>&#8221;)
contained in an operating agreement among HGEL and affiliates of Exxon Mobil Corporation (&#8220;<span style="text-decoration: underline">Exxon</span>&#8221;) and China National
Offshore Oil Corporation (&#8220;<span style="text-decoration: underline">CNOOC</span>&#8221;) regarding the Stabroek Block offshore Guyana.</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0 0 12pt">The arbitration merits hearing about the applicability of the Stabroek
ROFR to the Merger has been scheduled for May 2025, with a decision expected in the following three months. Chevron and Hess had expected
and requested that this hearing be held earlier, but the arbitrators&#8217; common schedules did not make this possible.</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0 0 12pt">The views of Chevron and Hess on the merits remain unchanged.
Exxon and CNOOC continue to ignore the plain language of the operating agreement, and Chevron and Hess remain confident that the
arbitration will confirm that the Stabroek ROFR does not apply to the Merger.</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0 0 12pt">Chevron and Hess remain committed to the Merger and look forward
to combining the two companies.</p>
