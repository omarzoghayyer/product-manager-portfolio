<TABLE CELLPADDING="0" CELLSPACING="0" STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin-top: 12pt; margin-bottom: 0pt"><TR STYLE="vertical-align: top">

<TD STYLE="width: 18pt">&nbsp;</TD><TD STYLE="width: 18pt"><FONT STYLE="font-family: Symbol">&#183;</FONT></TD><TD STYLE="text-align: left"><B>Drives industry-leading free cash flow growth and shareholder returns</B></TD></TR></TABLE>




<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 10pt 0pt"><B>HOUSTON, Texas, July 18, 20<FONT STYLE="color: windowtext">25</FONT></B>
&mdash; <FONT STYLE="color: windowtext">Chevron Corporation (NYSE: CVX) announced today that it has completed its acquisition of Hess
Corporation (NYSE: HES) following the satisfaction of all necessary closing conditions, including a favorable arbitration outcome regarding
Hess&rsquo; offshore Guyana asset. The combined company has one of the most advantaged and differentiated portfolios in the industry,
with leading positions in critical energy markets around the world and a high cash margin production profile. In addition, on July 17,
2025, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) lifted its earlier restriction, clearing the way for John Hess to join Chevron&rsquo;s Board
of Directors, subject to Board approval.</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0 10pt">&ldquo;This merger of two great American companies brings together
the best in the industry,&rdquo; said Chevron Chairman and CEO Mike Wirth. &ldquo;The combination enhances and extends our growth profile
well into the next decade, which we believe will drive greater long-term value to shareholders. Additionally, I&rsquo;m pleased with the
FTC&rsquo;s unanimous decision. John is a respected industry leader, and our Board would benefit from his experience, relationships and
expertise.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0 10pt">&ldquo;We are proud of everyone at Hess for building one of the industry&rsquo;s
best growth portfolios including Guyana, the world&rsquo;s largest oil discovery in the last 10 years, and the Bakken shale, where we
are a leading oil and gas producer,&rdquo; former Hess Corporation CEO John Hess said. &ldquo;The strategic combination of Chevron and
Hess creates a premier energy company positioned for the future.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0 10pt"><FONT STYLE="color: windowtext">The acquisition adds world class
assets, including Guyana and U.S. Bakken, to Chevron&rsquo;s diversified global portfolio where it is a leader in the Permian Basin, Gulf
of America, DJ Basin, Kazakhstan, Eastern Mediterranean and Australia. Chevron now owns a 30% position in the Guyana Stabroek Block, which
has more than </FONT><FONT STYLE="color: black">11</FONT> <FONT STYLE="color: windowtext">billion barrels of oil equivalent discovered
recoverable resource; </FONT><FONT STYLE="color: black">463 thousand </FONT><FONT STYLE="color: windowtext">net acres of high-quality
inventory in the Bakken; complementary assets in the Gulf of America with </FONT><FONT STYLE="color: black">31 </FONT><FONT STYLE="color: windowtext">thousand
barrels of oil equivalent per day; and natural gas assets in Southeast Asia with </FONT><FONT STYLE="color: black">57 </FONT><FONT STYLE="color: windowtext">thousand
barrels of oil equivalent per day.</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0 10pt">&ldquo;This accretive transaction is expected to drive significant
free cash flow and production growth into the 2030s,&rdquo; added Chief Financial Officer Eimear Bonner. &ldquo;We are quickly integrating
our two companies and expect to achieve $1 billion in annual run-rate cost synergies by the end of 2025. All of this should enable even
higher returns to shareholders over the long-term.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0 10pt">Under the terms of the merger agreement, Hess shareholders will receive
1.0250 shares of Chevron for each Hess share. As a result, Chevron intends to issue approximately 301 million shares of common stock out
of treasury to Hess stockholders in connection with the transaction. The 15.38 million shares of Hess common stock (which were acquired
in open market transactions) beneficially owned by Chevron immediately prior to the closing were cancelled for no consideration.</P>
